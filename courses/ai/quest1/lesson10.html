🌍 AI Ethics & Future

Congratulations! You've reached the final lesson of AI Quest 1. This lesson explores the ethical challenges and future possibilities of artificial intelligence.

"AI is a powerful tool – but with great power comes great responsibility. The choices we make today will shape the world of tomorrow."

⚖️ Key Ethical Principles

Transparency

AI systems should be explainable – users should understand how decisions are made.

Accountability

Someone must be responsible for AI outcomes – you can't blame the algorithm.

Fairness

AI should not discriminate based on race, gender, age, or other protected attributes.

Privacy

User data must be protected and used responsibly.

📋 Case Study: The COMPAS Recidivism Algorithm

COMPAS was a tool used by US courts to predict the likelihood of a criminal re‑offending. It was found to be biased against Black defendants – they were twice as likely to be falsely flagged as high‑risk compared to white defendants.

Key takeaway: Even well‑intentioned AI can perpetuate historical biases if the training data reflects societal inequalities.

💡 Question: How could this have been avoided? (Data auditing, fairness metrics, diverse teams, independent oversight.)

💭 Interactive Ethical Dilemma

Scenario: You're building an AI system to screen job applicants. Your dataset is 90% male. The model learns to favour male candidates. What do you do?

Balance the dataset by including more female applicants and retrain the model.
Leave the model as is – it's just reflecting the data.
Remove gender from the dataset entirely – but the model still learns from gender‑coded language (e.g., "competitive", "collaborative").

✅ Best approach: Balance the dataset. This addresses the root cause. Removing gender is also good, but it doesn't fix the underlying imbalance – the model may still infer gender from other patterns.

🚀 The Future of AI

  • AGI (Artificial General Intelligence) – AI that can perform any intellectual task that a human can. Still theoretical, but progress is accelerating.
  • AI in Healthcare – diagnosing diseases, drug discovery, personalised treatment.
  • AI in Climate – optimising energy use, predicting climate patterns, and designing sustainable solutions.
  • AI and Work – automation of repetitive tasks, but also new roles (AI ethics, prompt engineering, AI trainers).
  • AI Safety – ensuring AI systems are aligned with human values and cannot cause harm.

✨ Challenge: Write Your Ethical AI Statement

Write a short paragraph (50‑100 words) stating your personal commitment to building fair, transparent, and accountable AI systems.

💡 Example:

"I commit to building AI systems that are transparent, fair, and accountable. I will audit my data for bias, involve diverse stakeholders, and prioritise human well‑being over profit. I will never build AI that knowingly discriminates or harms."
